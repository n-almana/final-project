Proper Introductions

Hi, my name is Noora Al-Mana.
I never said this simple sentence just like that before I stepped on to this campus.
You are probably thinking that I'm lying because I had to have introduced myself before;
but I've actually never said it that way before.
My name is نوره بنت محمد بن فهد المانع (Noora bint Mohammed bin Fahad Al-Mana).
If that's too long I just go with نوره المانع (Noora Al-Mana).
I never had to worry about it being too difficult to pronounce,
too foreign to fit in,
too exotic to accept.
Except when I got here.

A slightly nuanced way of pronouncing my name suddenly slipped
out of my tongue in my first introduction in August of 2011.
Hi, my name is Noora Al-Mana.
I didn't think too much of it. That's still my name.
I'm just making it a little bit easier.
A little bit easier. (Easier for who? For me or for you?)
Why was it easy for me to drop an O in saying my name?
So easy that I started doing it without realizing it?

Maybe it's because it sounds so... Arabic
So... Middle Eastern
So... different
Yet dropping one letter can change that.
Or maybe it's because it reminds me of home and everything I left behind.
Or maybe it has nothing to do with that at all...

I quickly realized that this slight change was not my attempt to assimilate to the culture here.
That was never my intention or desire.
No. This was my desperate attempt to escape my past.
Something as core and integral as my name reminded me of who I was before coming here:
A girl that always did what she was told.
Obeyed all orders.
Never questioned anything.
Never spoke up when she disagreed.
A girl that was always afraid.

I hated that.
Something as trivial as pronouncing my name how I have always said it,
How everyone else have always said it,
How it should be said ‐
Reminded me of her.

I abandoned that girl 3 and a half years ago.
I left her in Qatar and went half way across the world.
I stopped saying her name.
I stopped introducing her to people.
I was too ashamed.

At least that's what I thought I did...

The truth is that girl is still here.
She always has been.
That girl grew to become someone even I didn't recognize.
I'm glad she didn't leave.
She is still here.
I am still here.
So hi, my name is Noora.
