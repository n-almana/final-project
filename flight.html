Flight

"Who do you love?" - YG
I was going there for its academic excellence
or its abundance of opportunities,
the land of the free
where people from all over the world convene
in pursuit of the American Dream.
Some of them working ‐
sometimes forever ‐ while their families waiting
to no avail. To die trying.
A girl could lose all hope in that place.

          I was going there
to attain some space from
all that brings both pain and joy,
always together. Simultaneously
holding me back and pushing me forward.
Others in my position ‐ never
making it back ‐ leaving their families.
In pursuit of what is supposed to matter: studies,
degrees, employment. Disguised flight in education.

                    I went there
a student, still studying for exams
trying to pass my courses, though really
learning the basics of life. (How else
am I supposed to grow?) My classmates
reading their books ‐ as their parents
told them to ‐ showing me what
must be done to obtain that which we all seek.

                              I stayed
four years though I never imagined
my completion. In my early days,
I never would have seen myself independent
across the ocean in pursuit of knowledge ‐
or was it peace? ‐ despite the distance
and my lost tongue
that still allowed the abroad
(student that I am) to feel at home.
